About Us

Who are you?

We're a couple hackers who wanted to make it easy to compare hospitals based on a couple simple benchmarks.

Aren't all hospitals the same?

No. Not even close. There are countless studies about how you're ten times as likely to survive surgery at one hospital versus another, and how medical staff are often scared to receive surgery at the hospital they work at because they see what goes on there. But statistics are boring. John Oliver can explain, in much more vivid detail, one particularly egregious problem.

Where does this data come from?

US government open data sets. Thanks Obama! Hospitals have their own data that is much more accurate than the data we use for this website but they keep it tightly under wraps because this somehow isn't illegal. Meanwhile, most hospital and doctor rating systems [cough] US News & World Report [cough] are based off of surveys instead of patient outcomes. In other words, it doesn't matter whether your patients survive the surgery they perform; what matters is the doctors' bedside manner in informing your relatives of your untimely death from an unnecessary surgery.

What algorithm do you use?

Out algorithm is still under development. We will publish it as soon as we can. The "Safety" score is based off of objective patient outcomes, such as survival rate. The "Comfort" score is based on subjective patient reviews. As you can see they don't often agree. In the meantime, if you're a specialist in the field and would like to provide input, please get in touch.
